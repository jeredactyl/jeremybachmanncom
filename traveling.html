I love exploring the world. Over the years, I've done deep travel in the United States, Mexico, India, and Ethiopia. Since COVID-19, I've committed to seeing as many new areas of the world as I can.

To that end, I've been using the 1301 world regions of NomadMania as my guide and inspiration.

Here are some of the airline tricks I've learned:

Southwest is a popular airline in my family. It flies out of my two local airports - BUR and LAX. Every hour, it flies to the Bay Area, where we have family and my daughters went to college. We don't have to price out the flight a la carte to see if we're getting a good deal (looking at you, Spirit Airlines). 2 bags included, no thinking involved.

Hack #1: Book your flight, then check the pricing regularly.

Southwest Airlines provide customers the ability to rebook their flight anytime between initial booking and 10 minutes before the flight leaves. You can receive the difference back in cash, Travel Funds, transferable flight credits, or points. Recently, I booked two tickets to Kansas City for a family event. I checked the price on southwest.com a few times and was able to shave $50 off each ticket a few weeks later.

Hack #2: Wanna Get Away Plus™

Credits can be transferred to anyone with a Rapid Rewarda account, not just for you. This is a great benefit for us, as someone in my family almost always has an upcoming Southwest flight. We can transfer credits between all four of us.

You can also make confirmed same-day flight changes without paying a fee. This is not only nice to get on an earlier or later flight if you want, it can be used to get you the flight you really want, if you're up for the risk. Just book the cheapest flight for that day - these are usually really early or late in the day. Then, at 12:00:01 after midnight, log on to Southwest.com and change the flight to the one you really want. But, if your ideal flight is booked, be prepared to get to the airport at the crack of dawn!

Hack #3: Southwest Gift Cards

Southwest gift cards pop up on sale at Best Buy, Target, and other places for 8% to 10% off all the time. There are some downsides - you won't earn the 3% on travel that Chase cards can give on travel, since these will code as regular shopping. Also, A Southwest gift card used for travel is not refundable. If you end up not taking the flight, the gift card amount becomes flight credit at Southwest.

We joke about Spirit all the time in my family, but we keep flying it when it's often so much less expensive than competing flights. You should always do the math to see if it's really cheaper after you add bags, though.

Update: Spirit recently ran a status match campaign that enabled me to upgrade my status to Free Spirit Gold®. On top of other benefits, this means no more bag fees while I hold the status. This makes Spirit a much more attractive option in many cases.

Hack #1 - Use WhatsApp or Text for Customer Service

If you call or use the chat function on the website, expect to sometimes wait more than an hour. I've had good luck getting an answer in minutes by texting "Hello" to (855) 728-3555.





Using Our iPhones Phone Abroad: A Guide for iPhone Users

Using your US-based phone abroad can be confusing and expensive. You can try to depend on public wifi, but you never know if it will be available wherever you end up. In this post, we will explore the most economical options for iPhone users planning a trip to Belize with a Verizon 5G Play More plan.

Verizon Travel Options

Verizon offers two international travel options for iPhone users: TravelPass and the International Monthly Travel Plan. The TravelPass costs $10/day per line, with unlimited data and texts. It offers 2GB of high-speed data before converting to 3G. The International Travel Plan costs $100/month, providing unlimited data and texts, with 20GB of high-speed data. For a 9-day trip to Belize, the total cost for TravelPass would be $360, while the International Travel Plan would cost $400.

Airalo eSIM

Airalo offers eSIMs for over 200 countries and regions. It caters to travelers who regularly visit other countries and want to use their phones. The eSIMs provided by Airalo are data-only, which means they do not have a phone number assigned and cannot make or receive calls or text messages; however, they do provide ultra-fast mobile data at nearly local prices.

Airalo utilizes multiple networks to provide service instead of building its own infrastructure. You can manage your eSIM plans through the Airalo app. Using Airalo eSIMs, we no longer need to change SIM cards or carry multiple SIM cards when going abroad. In Belize, their Cho! Google Fi

Google Fi is an option for international travel, but its terms state that it should be used primarily in the US and not just for international travel. To me, it looks like a great plan for digital nomads; I plan to keep my Verizon plan in the US for now.

Rely on Free WiFi

Another option is to rely on free WiFi at your destination. While this option is not always reliable or secure, it can be a cost-effective way to stay connected during your trip. I've tried to do this in the past in Ethiopia and France with sporadic, frustrating results. At the time I made those trips, high-speed access wasn't consistently available everywhere I went.
